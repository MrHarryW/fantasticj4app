GENERAL INFORMATION

Fantastic Beasts and Where to Find Them is a comprehensive field guide documenting the magical creatures that inhabit the wizarding world. Written by magizoologist Newt Scamander and J.K. Rowling, it serves both as a textbook at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry and as an authoritative reference for magical scholars.

The creatures featured in this book originate from regions across the globe, and many are well-hidden from non-magical eyes. By studying these beasts, readers gain a deeper appreciation for the richness and complexity of the magical world.

Newt Scamander’s work emphasizes the importance of conservation and ethical treatment of magical creatures, promoting peaceful coexistence between humans and the magical wildlife that surrounds them.

Beastiary

The majority of all creatures in the book.

Giant spider, capable of speech, extremely dangerous.

Ashwinder

Thin, pale-grey serpent born from magical fire.

Augurey

Greenish-black, mournful bird; its cry once thought to foretell death.

Basilisk

Giant serpent, deadly gaze; bred by Dark Wizards.

Billywig

Insect with stinger that induces giddiness and levitation.

Bowtruckle

Tree guardian creature; shy, eats insects.

Bundimun

Oozes sticky green substance; lives in dark places.

Centaur

Intelligent half-human, half-horse beings.

Chimaera

Extremely rare Greek monster with lion, goat, and dragon parts.

Chizpurfle

Crab-like parasites attracted to magic.

Clabbert

Tree-dwelling creature with pustule that glows red when sensing danger.

Crup

Dog-like beast with a forked tail; fiercely loyal to wizards.

Demiguise

Invisibility and precognition; resembles silver ape.

Diricawl

Disapparates when threatened; Muggles call it the dodo.

Doxy

Also known as biting fairies, small and dangerous.

Dragon

(Breeds include Antipodean Opaleye, Chinese Fireball, Common Welsh Green, Hebridean Black, Hungarian Horntail, Norwegian Ridgeback, Peruvian Vipertooth, Romanian Longhorn, Swedish Short‑Snout, Ukrainian Ironbelly) – Fire‑breathing, powerful, and highly regulated.

Dugbog

Marsh creature resembling a crocodile resting on logs.

Erkling

A German goblin‑like creature that sings children away.

Erumpent

Huge rhino-like beast, explodes what it pierces.

Fairy

Small winged humanoids; often mischievous.

Fire Crab

Red crustacean that spits sparks; native to Asia.

Flobberworm

Fwooper

Colorful bird whose song can drive people insane.

Ghoul

Ugly creature living in attics; not truly dangerous.

Glumbumble

Puff-headed insect; causes sadness in its wake.

Gnome

Small garden beast; harmless.

Graphorn

Mountain-dwelling, aggressive with golden horns.

Griffin

Eagle-lion hybrid; proud and fierce.

Grindylow

Water demon, green and aggressive.

Hidebehind

Nocturnal forest creature; hides from humans.

Hippocampus

Seahorse-like aquatic creature found in the Black Lake.

Hippogriff

Eagle-horse hybrid, proud and powerful.

Hodag

Reptilian beast known from North American folklore.

Horklump

Round pink fungus-like creature.

Horned Serpent

American magical snake with venomous horn.

Imp

Small mischievous magical creature known for causing minor trouble and pranks.

Incubus

Male demon that seduces women in their sleep, known in wizarding folklore.

Jarvey

Magical creature resembling a large ferret, known for its loud and unpleasant chatter.

Jobberknoll

Tiny blue bird that remains silent while alive but reveals all it sees when it dies.

Kappa

Water demon from Japanese folklore with a hollow filled with water on its head.

Kelpie

Shape-shifting water spirit often appearing as a horse, luring victims to watery graves.

Knarl

Hedgehog-like creature known for its aggressive behavior when threatened.

Leprechaun

Small Irish fairy known for mischief and guarding pots of gold.

Lethifold

Also known as the Living Shroud, a dark, murderous creature that suffocates victims.

Lilith

Rare vampiric creature from European mythology, known for its haunting presence.

Lobalug

Small swamp-dwelling creature with a colorful shell, mostly harmless.

Loch Ness Monster

Mysterious aquatic creature rumored to inhabit Scotland’s Loch Ness.

Manticore

Fearsome beast with the body of a lion, human head, and a venomous tail.

Merpeople

Humanoid aquatic creatures living in underwater communities with their own languages.

Moke

Small lizard-like creature capable of shrinking to fit in pockets; known for its shiny, protective skin.

Niffler

Small, furry creature attracted to shiny things, often causing mischief while searching for treasure.

Nogtail

Beast resembling a small dragon with a bony tail, known for its territorial nature.

Occamy

Winged serpentine creature that can grow or shrink to fit available space; lays multicolored eggs.

Owl

Common magical pet and messenger in the wizarding world, known for their intelligence and loyalty.

Phoenix

Majestic bird that can regenerate by bursting into flames and rising from its ashes.

Pigmy Puff

Small, fluffy magical creature kept as a pet, known for its cute appearance.

Tiny, mischievous flying creatures that love to cause chaos.

Small horse-like creature, often used as a companion by witches and wizards.

Beast with five legs, known for its odd gait and aggressive temperament.

Small, murderous goblin that dyes its hat with the blood of its victims.

Three-headed snake known for its dangerous venom and distinctive scales.

Salamander

Fire-dwelling creature that can live in flames without harm.

Scurrier

Small, fast, and elusive creature often found around wizarding communities.

Mythical creature with a lion’s body and human head, known for riddles and wisdom.

Thunderbird

Large, majestic bird that can create storms as it flies.

Troll

Large, brutish creature often found under bridges or in caves.

Unicorn

Beautiful, pure horse-like creature with a spiraled horn; symbolizes purity and grace.

Veela

Magical beings known for their enchanting beauty and ability to entrance humans.

Werewolf

Human who transforms into a wolf during a full moon, often struggling with the curse.

Wampus Cat

Magical feline with incredible strength and agility, native to North America.

Yeti

Large ape-like creature said to inhabit the Himalayan mountains.

Zouwu

Massive feline beast known for its incredible speed and ability to travel great distances.
